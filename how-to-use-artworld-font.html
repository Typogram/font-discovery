Meet Artworld: A Striking Display Typeface with Vintage Flair

If you enjoyed this post, subscribe to get updates from our blog!

Artworld is a bold, nostalgic display typeface that captures the vibrant, expressive spirit of 1980s graphic design. Originally a rub-down lettering style, it's now digitally preserved for modern creators looking to channel vintage flair.

Type Details

  • Display typeface inspired by 1980s rub-down lettering

  • Beveled letterforms with sculpted light-and-shadow effects

  • Editable internal shapes for custom dimensional styling

Artworld is ideal for album covers, event posters, fashion branding, or a “vintage cool”. Its sleek beveled edges and sculpted light-and-shadow effects channel the nostalgia of a bygone design era, while adding a fresh, modern twist. Artworld is a rare gem that seamlessly blends vintage charm with modern versatility. It’s one of those unique fonts that’s perfect for that special project. My favorite feature is the ability to select and style individual shapes within each letter, then experiment with colors and shadows for added dimensionality. 

Behind the Typeface

Originally released in 1984 by French company Mecanorma, Artworld comes from a rich history of rub-down lettering, a once-essential tool for graphic designers. In the early 2000s, International TypeFounders (ITF) revived this lettering legacy by digitizing the Mecanorma Collection, preserving a powerful archive of expressive display styles. Artworld stands out as one of its gems.

Using Artworld for Typography and Design

Artworld brings instant attitude to any project. Whether you're crafting a retro-inspired album cover, a bold fashion campaign, or a poster that needs to stop someone in their tracks, this typeface delivers standout impact. Its dimensional details and nostalgic energy make it a go-to for typographers and designers who want their work to feel expressive and new. Pair it with simple sans serifs or minimal layouts to let the letters truly shine.
