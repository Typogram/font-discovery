Six Caps is a sans serif. A quick reminder: sans serifs are letterforms without the little serif(feet). As you might have noticed, this sans serif appears visibly narrower than regular fonts. This is called condensed font.Â 

It is very common to find condensed fonts sitting inside classified sections in newspapers or magazines. Due to its width, a condensed font is more visually pleasing sometimes and can best leverage a narrow space. When we talk about the personalities of fonts, the most telling characteristics are in their lowercase. For our case, Six Caps is relatively neutral and doesn't have a traditional lowercase. Instead, it has a small-cap to meet all your spacing needs. If you need to place text in a tight space, like the corners of a photo, use a condensed font like Six Caps can be incredibly useful.Â
